Happiness

How does an average person’s degree of happiness correlate with factors such as their financial earning power and living environment?

World Happiness Score

The World Happiness Score is a measure of the degree of happiness of countries, compiled in an annual report by a team of economists, formed through a United Nations global initiative Sustainable Development Solutions Network [4].

The measure is primarily based on the survey data collection efforts of the annual Gallup World Poll [5], based on the survey answers of representative annual samples of typically 1000 respondents from each countries’ population. Survey question topics are those closely pertaining to the main life evaluations of respondents [6].

Fragile States Index

The Fragile States Index [1] is a ranking measure of the stability status of countries, devised by a United States non-governmental, non-profit organisation Fund for Peace [2].

It is the result of a combination of human experts analysis and data collection using advanced information technology techniques. Countries are ranked by political analysts according to 12 key indicators in areas of political, social and economic stability, as well as degrees of internal cohesion and external interventions [3].

GDP per capita (PPP)

GDP of a country is an economic measure of the total monetary value of final services and goods produced by the country’s whole population over a period of time, in terms of the country’s national currency [7]. A country with a high GDP can have a low-moderate average standard of living, and vice versa (for example, China versus Singapore).

In order to properly compare different countries’ standard of living, economists usually refer to the GDP per capita (PPP), which is basically GDP averaged by the country population and converted to a common currency (typically, US dollars) using the Purchasing Power Parity (PPP) exchange rate [8].
